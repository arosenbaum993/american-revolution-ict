Question 1  (1 point • Multiple Choice)

B. Britain had no debt after the French and Indian War and therefore lowered taxes in the colonies.
C. Colonists objected to being taxed by a Parliament in which they had no elected representatives.
D. Colonists believed the British king should have more power over their legislatures.

Question 3  (1 point • Selected Response)
From Source 3: The video suggests that one reason the American Revolution “matters” today is:
A. That it only changed military tactics and not political ideas.
B. That more people are ruled by monarchs now because of the Revolution.

Research Question: To what extent did the American Revolution succeed in achieving its founding ideals?

Purpose: Argue a position about the success and limitations of the Revolution.
Audience: Your classmates and teacher.

State your claim clearly, use evidence from all sources, and address a counterclaim.
  • Provide an introduction that guides the focus and captures the audience.
  • Clearly establish my claim(s) and position on the topic.
  • Develop a cohesive and effective organizational structure.
  • Use a style consistently appropriate to establish credibility and tone.
  • Support my reasons with ideas and evidence from multiple sources.
  • Credit sources when integrating quotes or paraphrased ideas.
  • Expand on ideas using multiple techniques and rhetorical devices or appeals.
  • Use varied transitions to connect reasons to claim(s).
  • Provide a strong conclusion.
  • Use varied syntax to clearly convey ideas.
  • Check for errors in spelling, capitalization, punctuation, usage, or grammar.
